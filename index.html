The Significance of Swathi

Swathi is the Avatara Nakshatra (birth star) of Lord Sri Lakshmi Narasimha. In Ahobilam, this day is celebrated every month with divine energy. Devotees gather to witness the special Alankara and Sevas performed to the Lord.

1. Nava Narasimha Abhishekam

On the day of Swathi, sacred Abhishekam is performed at all 9 Narasimha Temples. This ritual involves bathing the deities with milk, honey, and curd while chanting powerful Vedic hymns.

The Pinnacle of Devotion & Adventure

The Divine Ascent Begins

The trek to Ugra Stambham commences from Upper Ahobilam. This is not just a journey; it is an adventure through the heart of the Nallamala forest. As you climb the rugged rocky paths, the rhythmic sound of nearby waterfalls and the chirping of wild birds create a meditative atmosphere that prepares your soul for the ultimate darshan.

Starting Point: Upper Ahobilam (Near Jwala Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple)

Safety First: Preparation for the Trek

This trek is steep and physically demanding. It is advised that children, elderly people, and those with health issues avoid this climb. To ensure a safe journey, wearing Grip-Enhanced Trekking Shoes is compulsory, as the rocks can be extremely slippery.

Must CarryWater Bottles & Energetic Snacks
FootwearTrekking/Hiking Shoes Only

At the Summit: A Vision of Paradise

Reaching the peak makes every struggle worth it. You are greeted by an endless view of Lush Green Hills and deep valleys that stretch into the horizon. The cool breeze passing through the narrow cliffs feels like the divine touch of the Lord Himself. Standing at the edge of this majestic cliff, the tranquility of nature will leave you in awe and spiritual bliss.
